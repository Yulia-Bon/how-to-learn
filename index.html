Main Problems in Learning

Neither in school nor in university are we taught how to properly study material. We prepare for exams and memorize answers. We practice solving repetitive tasks to do better on tests, but often, in the end, this does not give us real knowledge. Rote learning quickly fades away and brings no lasting benefit.

Conclusion: The ability to learn is also something that needs to be taught, but for some reason, it is rarely taught. What can be done about this?

Specific techniques and exercises help change the approach to learning, making it more effective and engaging. These techniques are also applied in the example of learning in the Practicum.

Learning Techniques

Five Practices by Barbara Oakley

Two Types of Attention

Deep knowledge emerges when focused and diffuse thinking are alternated.

Recall

Recall what you've learned — this will help connect scattered pieces of memory.

Interleaving

Learn several skills simultaneously, as they enrich each other.

Questions

While listening to the instructor, come up with a good question that really concerns you.

Illusion of Competence

Repeat to yourself, write it down, or explain it to a friend: it only seems like you’ve mastered a new topic.

The Story of Barbara Oakley

Since childhood, Barbara struggled with mathematics. She considered herself a complete humanities person, and not the smartest one at that. In the army, she studied Russian to earn a bonus, and she did so successfully, which led to her being promoted to a commanding position. But to advance in her career, she had to pass mathematics. So Barbara came up with her own approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if something is hard for you, the knowledge you acquire through effort is much deeper than for those who understand things at first glance.
